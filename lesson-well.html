The Samaritan Woman

An encounter at the well, where Jesus speaks to the unseen, the weary, and the deeply known.

The Story

She comes to the well at noon not because she prefers the heat, but because she prefers solitude. The whispers are quieter then. The stares fewer. The judgment less visible.

Jesus is already there. Tired. Thirsty. Waiting. He speaks first, crossing cultural lines, religious rules, and personal shame with one simple request: “Will you give Me a drink?”

“When a Samaritan woman came to draw water, Jesus said to her, ‘Will you give me a drink?’”
— John 4:7

Jesus’ Heart

Jesus does not expose her to shame her. He reveals her to restore her. He names her truth gently, without accusation, without disgust. He stays. He listens. He invites.

“The hour is coming, and is now here, when the true worshipers will worship the Father in spirit and truth.”
— John 4:23

Her Heart

She begins guarded. Defensive. Curious. But as He speaks, something softens. She realizes: He knows me and He is still talking to me.

Reflection

  • Where do I avoid being seen and why?
  • What truth has Jesus been gently naming in my life?
  • What would it look like to stay at the well with Him?
